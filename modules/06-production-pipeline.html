STAGE 06 · LAB INTEGRATION

From TLI upload to cycle live-birth estimate

Ingest supports EmbryoScope, Geri, and Miri (direct API) or manual upload. Inference runs on cloud GPU with per-clinic data isolation; a cycle-level Lambda computes live-birth probability once all embryos are scored and pushes the result over WebSocket to the lab dashboard or LIMS (HL7 FHIR).

STEP 01 · CLINIC INGEST
EmbryoScope / Geri / Miri → Rhea gateway

Direct integration with Vitrolife EmbryoScope (via EmbryoViewer export), Genea Biomedx Geri (via Geri Connect API), Esco Miri TL. Manual upload is also supported. TLI videos undergo automated frame selection (gradient analysis + HPI heuristics) to pick the representative Day-5 frame.

↓
STEP 02 · QUEUE
8-way fan-out on Amazon SQS FIFO

Each image is dispatched to 8 fold-specific SQS queues. Messages are processed in cycle-order to preserve clinical chronology. Each clinic’s data is strictly isolated at the queue and S3 prefix level.

↓
STEP 03 · INFERENCE
EC2 GPU — MobileNetV2 + DoodleEncoder + ViT

Inference executes at 256×256 RGB on G5-class GPU. Mean of 8 folds = p_implantation. STD across the 256 Monte-Carlo passes = confidence. Typical per-embryo latency: < 4s.

↓
STEP 04 · ANEUPLOIDY RISK
Age-conditioned parametric estimate

Where PGT-A has not been performed, the pipeline adds an age-based aneuploidy prior derived from published reference curves: PSigmoid(0.20, 0.90, 35.0, 0.25). This is not a substitute for PGT-A; it is a population prior that helps calibrate cycle-level expectations.

↓
STEP 05 · CYCLE-LEVEL MODEL
AWS Lambda — live-birth probability

When all embryos for the cycle have scored, a Lambda combines per-embryo p_implantation with the age-conditioned fetal-heartbeat-to-live-birth factor FHB2LB to produce a single cycle live-birth probability, plus high / mid / low confidence bands.

↓
STEP 06 · DELIVERY
WebSocket to dashboard / LIMS

Results are pushed in real time via WebSocket to the lab dashboard. A signed JSON payload is also delivered to the clinic’s LIMS (HL7 FHIR-compatible) for durable record-keeping and downstream reporting.

Infrastructure · Versioning, auto-scaling, data isolation

The EC2 inference service auto-hibernates after 10 minutes of inactivity and cold-starts on the first queued message, minimizing idle cost for low-volume clinics. Model weights are versioned in S3 (e.g. weights/2024-12-31.zip) with hot-swap: the service checks for updates hourly and rolls over to the new checkpoint without interrupting in-flight inference.

Each clinic operates in its own data partition (S3 prefix, SQS queue group, Postgres schema). No embryo image leaves the clinic’s region of residency. For the GenPrime clinics, inference runs in AWS ap-southeast-1 (Singapore).

Cycle-level model · Live-birth probability
p_overall = mean(p_implantation1, p_implantation2, …, p_implantationn)
p_livebirth = 1 − ∏i(1 − FHB2LB(age) · pi)
FHB2LB(age) = sigmoid(offset=0, max=0.875, mid=44.96, slope=−0.464)

FHB2LB is the age-conditioned probability that a fetal heartbeat at 5–8 weeks proceeds to live birth, fit to Rhea outcome data. The primary endpoint is live birth, not clinical pregnancy — so the cycle number reported to the patient is lower than a clinical-pregnancy figure, but it is the number that matches the outcome they care about.
