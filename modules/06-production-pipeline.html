STAGE 06

From Image to Insight

In production, the Bonna pipeline runs on EC2 with GPU acceleration, processing embryo images through an SQS queue and computing cycle-level live birth probabilities.

RUNNING EXAMPLE Embryo #47 — Day 5 Blastocyst Patient: 34yo, AMH 2.1 ng/mL, BMI 24.2

In production: the clinic uploads Embryo #47's image via the GenPrime web interface. It's queued to 8 SQS fold-specific queues. EC2 returns p_implantation = 72%, s_implantation = 5%, p_aneuploidy = 22% (age 34). When the cycle's 6 other embryos finish scoring, the Lambda computes: p_livebirth = 1 - ∏(1 - 0.875 × pᵢ) = 84% for the full cycle. A cycle_done WebSocket event fires to the clinic dashboard.

STEP 01
CLINIC UPLOAD

Day-5 blastocyst image or TLI video uploaded via web interface. Videos undergo frame selection (gradient analysis, HPI heuristics).

STEP 02
SQS QUEUE

Image queued to 8 fold-specific SQS queues for parallel inference across the ensemble.

STEP 03
EC2 GPU INFERENCE

MobileNetV2 + DoodleP2P + ViT ensemble processes at 256×256 RGB. Mean of 8 folds = p_implantation. STD = confidence.

STEP 04
ANEUPLOIDY ESTIMATION

Age-based parametric sigmoid: 20% at young ages → 90% at advanced maternal age. PSigmoid(0.20, 0.90, 35.0, 0.25).

STEP 05
CYCLE MODEL (Lambda)

When all embryos scored: p_livebirth = 1 - ∏(1 - FHB2LB × p_i). Computes high/mid/low confidence bands by age.

STEP 06
WEBSOCKET NOTIFY

Real-time cycle_done event pushes results to clinic dashboard.

AUTO-SCALING INFRASTRUCTURE

The EC2 instance auto-hibernates after 10 minutes of no messages, minimizing cost. Model weights (2024-12-31.zip) are versioned in S3 with hot-swap capability — the service checks for updates hourly and restarts seamlessly. Each clinic’s data is isolated and processed in-order via SQS FIFO.

LIVE BIRTH PROBABILITY — THE MATH
p_overall = mean(p_implantation1, p_implantation2, …, p_implantationn)
p_livebirth = 1 - ∏i(1 - FHB2LB(age) × pi)
FHB2LB(age) = sigmoid(0, 0.875, 44.96, -0.464)
