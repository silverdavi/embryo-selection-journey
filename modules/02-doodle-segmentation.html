Teaching Machines to See

The first step: train a custom GAN to segment biological structures that define embryo quality — the zona pellucida, blastocyst cavity, and inner cell mass.

RAW EMBRYOSCOPE IMAGE — 500×500px, Day 5 blastocyst

DOODLEP2P OUTPUT — 3-channel biological segmentation mask

HOW DOODLEP2P WORKS
  1. Manual segmentation of 3,217 embryo frames at various developmental stages to create training labels
  2. A custom Pix2Pix GAN (DoodleP2P) learns to map raw microscopy images to 3-channel masks: zona pellucida (outer shell), cells (inner mass), and blastocyst cavity
  3. The pseudo-coloring normalizes optical variations across clinics, hardware, and imaging conditions — making the model robust to multi-center deployment
  4. A dedicated encoder (DoodleEncoder) extracts structural features from the segmentation mask for downstream classification
WHY SEGMENTATION MATTERS

By removing optical artifacts — brightness, light source, shadow, zoom scale — the segmentation step makes Bonna hardware-agnostic. The same model works on EmbryoScope data from Israel, Spain, Ukraine, and France without retraining.
