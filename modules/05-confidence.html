How uncertainty is computed · MC dropout + TTA
1
Test-time augmentation (TTA)

Each embryo frame is evaluated under 16 random transforms — rotation, translation, scaling, horizontal flip, brightness jitter. A well-trained model should not change its mind when the embryo is rotated 17°; if it does, that is epistemic uncertainty.

2
Monte-Carlo dropout (MCD)

For each augmented view, 16 forward passes apply independent dropout masks to the classifier heads. This approximates sampling from a Bayesian posterior over model parameters (Gal & Ghahramani, ICML 2016).

3
Confidence score

The standard deviation of the resulting 256-value distribution quantifies total predictive uncertainty. We report -log(σ) so that “higher is more certain” matches clinician intuition.

HOW TO USE THIS IN YOUR WORKFLOW

High confidence tier (top 10%): AUC ≈ 0.90. The model is internally consistent. Use the score as a primary ranking signal for this cycle’s transfer decision.

Mid tier (middle 50%): AUC ≈ 0.78. Typical case. Use the score alongside morphokinetic and patient factors.

Low-confidence tier (bottom 10%): The model is telling you it cannot reliably stratify this embryo from its view — common in cases with debris, off-focus frames, or unusual morphology. Weight embryologist judgment more heavily.
