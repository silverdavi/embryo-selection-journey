HOW CONFIDENCE SCORING WORKS
1
Augmentation

Each embryo image undergoes 16 random transformations (rotation, translation, scaling, flips) to test prediction stability across orientations.

2
Dropout simulation

For each augmented image, 16 dropout passes randomly omit subsets of neurons, simulating an ensemble of 256 different models evaluating the same embryo.

3
Confidence quantification

The standard deviation of the resulting 256 scores measures prediction stability. -log(STD) converts this to a confidence score where higher values mean greater certainty.

CLINICAL IMPLICATIONS

This isn't academic nuance — it's decision support. When Bonna reports high confidence, AUC approaches 0.9 (near-diagnostic accuracy). When confidence is low, the clinician knows to weigh other factors more heavily. The model becomes a calibrated collaborator, not a black-box oracle.
