STAGE 05 · UNCERTAINTY QUANTIFICATION

Per-embryo uncertainty, reported alongside every score

Every embryo is evaluated in 256 forward passes (16 augmentations × 16 dropout masks). Bonna returns the mean (p_implantation) and a confidence score derived from the standard deviation. On the top-10% confidence stratum, AUC rises to 0.90; on the bottom stratum it drops toward chance, and the model signals this directly.

How uncertainty is computed · MC dropout + TTA
1
Test-time augmentation (TTA)

Each frame is evaluated under 16 random transforms (rotation, translation, scaling, horizontal flip, brightness jitter). Score variance across these transforms captures how dependent the prediction is on viewpoint.

2
Monte-Carlo dropout (MCD)

For each augmented view, 16 forward passes apply independent dropout masks to the classifier heads. This approximates sampling from a Bayesian posterior over model parameters (Gal & Ghahramani, ICML 2016).

3
Confidence score

The standard deviation of the resulting 256-value distribution quantifies total predictive uncertainty. We report -log(σ) so that “higher is more certain” matches clinician intuition.

READING THE TIERS

Top 10% (high confidence): AUC ≈ 0.90. Score is robust across 256 perturbations; usable as a primary ranking signal.

Middle 50%: AUC ≈ 0.78. Score should be weighed against morphokinetic and patient factors.

Bottom 10% (low confidence): Score is not robust on this frame — common with debris, off-focus frames, or unusual morphology. Defer to embryologist judgment.
