STAGE 01 · FOR THE EMBRYOLOGIST

Interobserver variability in Day 5 blastocyst assessment

A blinded panel of 36 senior embryologists scored the same 136 blastocysts. Mean accuracy was 51.9%. On fair-quality embryos (Gardner BB) Fleiss’ κ fell to 0.25. Years of experience, clinic volume, country, and degree did not predict who scored better. That is the problem Bonna addresses.

Case #47 is a morphologically borderline Gardner BB blastocyst. Full expansion, fair-to-good ICM, fair-to-good TE. When we submitted the raw time-lapse video to the Human Reproduction 2022 panel of 36 embryologists, it received IPG scores from 2 to 4 (on a 1–5 scale). Eleven panelists said “don’t transfer,” 25 said “transfer.” Kappa agreement on cases like this: κ = 0.25. The actual outcome: FHB at 7 weeks, live birth.

Study design · Fordham et al., Human Reproduction 2022

A blinded panel of 36 senior embryologists from 13 countries scored 136 time-lapse blastocyst videos with known implantation data (KID). Each embryologist independently assigned an Implantation Probability Grade (IPG) from 1 (very low) to 5 (very high), together with a binary transfer recommendation.

Agreement was stratified by embryo quality:

Quality stratumGardner gradeFleiss’ κInterpretation
Clearly poorCC / arrest0.65Substantial agreement
Fair / borderlineBB / BC / CB0.25Fair agreement only
Clearly goodAA / AB / BA0.65Substantial agreement

Fair-quality blastocysts are where most single-embryo transfer decisions live, and where embryologists disagree most.

WHAT THE DATA RULES OUT

In multivariable analysis, no embryologist characteristic — years of experience, clinic volume, country, highest degree, or preferred grading system — significantly predicted accuracy. The variability is not a training problem. It is inherent to subjective visual assessment of a continuous biological signal.

— Fordham DE, Silver DH, et al. Hum Reprod, 2022
