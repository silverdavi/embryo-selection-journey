The Three-Branch Ensemble

The Bonna algorithm fuses three deep learning approaches — each capturing different aspects of embryo morphology — into a single prediction through an 8-fold ensemble.

RUNNING EXAMPLE Embryo #47 — Day 5 Blastocyst Patient: 34yo, AMH 2.1 ng/mL, BMI 24.2

Embryo #47's image and its DoodleP2P mask flow through three parallel branches. MobileNetV2 extracts texture and edge features from the raw 256×256 pixel input. The DoodleEncoder processes the segmentation mask, focusing on zona thickness and cavity expansion. The Vision Transformer captures global spatial relationships — how the inner cell mass is positioned relative to the cavity. All three feature vectors are concatenated and passed through 8 independent MLP heads.

INFERENCE-TIME AUGMENTATION

Each image undergoes 16 random augmentations × 16 dropout simulations = 256 forward passes per embryo. This Monte Carlo approach produces a score distribution whose standard deviation quantifies prediction uncertainty — enabling clinically meaningful confidence reporting.
