STAGE 03 · MODEL ARCHITECTURE

Three views of the same blastocyst, one calibrated prediction

Bonna is not a single model. It is an 8-fold ensemble of three complementary views — pixels, biology, and global spatial pattern — concatenated and trained on Known Implantation Data (KID) from four countries.

Case · Inside the model Case #47 · Day 5 Blastocyst Gardner 4BB · Full expansion, fair ICM, fair TE
STAGE 03

For Case #47, the three branches produce distinct readings. MobileNetV2 (pixel branch) flags fair TE cell cohesion from local texture. DoodleEncoder (biology branch) measures a 73 µm mean zona thickness and a fully-expanded cavity. ViT (global branch) captures the ICM’s mildly off-centered position — which correlates weakly but meaningfully with implantation in our training corpus. The 8 MLP heads return a distribution of probabilities; their mean and standard deviation become p_implantation and the confidence score.

MEAN OF 8 FOLDS × 256 PASSES
Confidence
-log(STD) OF 256 PASSES
Inference-time augmentation · Uncertainty quantification

Each embryo frame is evaluated in 256 forward passes: 16 random augmentations (rotations, translations, horizontal flips, brightness jitter) × 16 Monte-Carlo dropout samples. This is MCD — Monte-Carlo Dropout as proposed by Gal & Ghahramani (2016) — applied to a medical imaging problem.

The resulting 256-element score distribution has a mean (the implantation probability) and a standard deviation (the uncertainty). The clinician receives both. A tight distribution means the model is consistent across orientations and parameter subsets; a wide distribution tells you to weigh your own judgment more heavily.
